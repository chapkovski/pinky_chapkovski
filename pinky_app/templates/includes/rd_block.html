You will now be asked to make a decision that could affect both your earnings as well as the {% op %}’s earnings. Note the other two {% bps %} are presented with the same decision.
You have the choice to affect the {% op %}’s earnings, at a cost to you. Specifically:
  • You can increase the {% op %}’s earnings by ${{ Constants.rp_coef }} for every $1 you give up
  • Or, you can decrease the {% op %}’s earnings by ${{ Constants.rp_coef }} for every $1 you give up
  • Or, you can choose to not affect (no change) the {% op %}’s earnings by NOT giving up any of your Stage 1 earnings.
After all {% bps %}make their decision, one of the three {% bps %} will be selected randomly to have their decision implemented which may affect both their earnings and the {% op %}’s earnings. Thus, your choice has a 1 in 3 chance of actually occurring.
  • If your choice is selected, your earnings as well as the {% op %}’s earnings will be affected by your decision below.
  • If your choice is not selected, neither your earnings nor the {% op %}’s earnings will be affected by your decision below.
  • NOTE: The {% op %} will receive, at a minimum, their show-up fee.
  • NOTE: you can only give up dollars up to your Stage 1 earnings.
The {% op %} knows that the {% bps %}’ actions may affect their earnings, however they do NOT know the specific decision you are making or which {% bp %} affected their earnings. They will only be informed whether and by how much their earnings have increased, decreased, or remained the same.
